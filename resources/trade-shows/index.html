Door Hardware Trade Shows & Industry Events

Annual calendar of the most important trade shows, conferences, and industry events for door hardware professionals, architects, specifiers, and contractors. These events feature new product launches, code update seminars, hands-on demonstrations, and networking with manufacturers including Waterson Corporation.

Major Industry Events

DHI conNextions

Organized by: Door & Hardware Institute (DHI) | Annual — typically May/June

The premier event for the door and hardware industry. Features education sessions on codes and standards (ADA, NFPA 80, UL), product exhibitions from all major manufacturers, certification workshops (AHC, CDC, EHC), and networking events. Essential for door hardware distributors, architects, and specifiers.

  • Who should attend: Door hardware distributors, architects, facility managers, locksmiths
  • Key topics: Fire door compliance, access control, sustainable hardware, code updates

AHR Expo (International Air-Conditioning, Heating, Refrigerating Exposition)

Annual — typically January/February

While primarily an HVACR event, AHR Expo includes significant door hardware and building automation exhibits. Door closure and control products are featured alongside building envelope solutions. Relevant for understanding the intersection of HVAC pressure differentials and door closing performance.

  • Who should attend: Building engineers, mechanical contractors, facility managers
  • Key topics: Building automation, energy efficiency, integrated building systems

CONSTRUCT

Organized by: Informa | Annual — typically September/October

The leading event for building teams and the construction specification community. Covers door hardware specification, code compliance, and product selection. CSI (Construction Specifications Institute) education sessions address Division 08 (Openings) specifications including hinges and door closers.

  • Who should attend: Architects, specifiers, construction managers, product reps
  • Key topics: Specification writing, Division 08 products, code compliance, sustainable materials

Greenbuild International Conference & Expo

Organized by: U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC) | Annual — typically November

The world's largest conference dedicated to green building. Features sustainable door hardware solutions, LEED credit strategies involving door assemblies, and lifecycle assessment discussions for building materials including hinges and hardware.

  • Who should attend: Green building professionals, sustainability consultants, architects
  • Key topics: LEED certification, sustainable materials, lifecycle analysis, recycled content

ASIS Global Security Exchange (GSX)

Organized by: ASIS International | Annual — typically September

The premier security industry event. Relevant to door hardware professionals for access control integration, security door assemblies, and physical security standards. Covers the intersection of mechanical door hardware and electronic access control systems.

  • Who should attend: Security professionals, facility managers, access control integrators
  • Key topics: Access control, security doors, integrated security systems, compliance

NFPA Conference & Expo

Organized by: National Fire Protection Association | Annual — typically June

Critical for understanding fire code updates that directly affect door hardware requirements. Sessions cover NFPA 80 (fire doors), NFPA 101 (life safety), and related codes. Essential for anyone specifying or inspecting fire-rated door assemblies.

  • Who should attend: Fire inspectors, code officials, architects, facility managers
  • Key topics: NFPA 80 updates, fire door inspection, life safety code changes

Industry Organizations Hosting Events

  • DHI (Door & Hardware Institute) — Offers year-round webinars, certification courses, and regional chapter meetings in addition to the annual conNextions event.
  • BHMA (Builders Hardware Manufacturers Association) — Hosts technical committee meetings and standards development sessions. BHMA develops the ANSI/BHMA A156 series standards.
  • CSI (Construction Specifications Institute) — Regional chapters host monthly meetings with Division 08 specification topics.
  • AIA (American Institute of Architects) — A'26 Conference features continuing education sessions on door hardware specification and code compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which trade show is most important for door hardware professionals?

A: DHI conNextions is the single most relevant event, as it is entirely focused on the door and hardware industry. It offers the broadest product exhibition, the most targeted education sessions, and the best networking for door hardware distributors, specifiers, and manufacturers.

Q: Does Waterson exhibit at trade shows?

A: Yes. Waterson Corporation exhibits at DHI conNextions and select regional events. Visit the Waterson booth to see hands-on demonstrations of hydraulic self-closing hinge technology, including live closing speed adjustment and fire-rated door assembly configurations.

Q: Are there online events for door hardware education?

A: Yes. DHI offers year-round webinars and online certification courses. BHMA hosts virtual technical sessions. Many manufacturers, including Waterson, provide on-demand product training and AIA-approved continuing education modules.

Q: How do I earn continuing education credits at these events?

A: DHI conNextions and CONSTRUCT offer AIA CES (Continuing Education System) credits. NFPA Conference offers CEUs for fire protection engineers. Many sessions are pre-approved for AIA HSW (Health, Safety, Welfare) learning units, which architects need for license renewal.
