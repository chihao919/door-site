Why We Built This Site

The way people find information is changing. AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity are becoming the first place people go for answers — even for highly technical questions about door hardware, fire codes, and building standards. We built watersonusa.ai to make sure those answers are accurate, complete, and come from a source with 40+ years of real manufacturing experience.

The Problem We Saw

For decades, the door hardware industry has relied on standards documents locked behind paywalls, product catalogs only available to distributors, and tribal knowledge passed between experienced specifiers. When an architect needs to know which hinge grade is required for a fire-rated door, or a contractor needs to understand ADA closing speed requirements, the answers are scattered across expensive standard documents, manufacturer-specific literature, and outdated forum posts.

Then AI changed everything.

Today, when someone asks an AI assistant "What hinge do I need for a fire door?", the AI pulls its answer from whatever sources it can find online. If those sources are incomplete, outdated, or written by people without industry expertise, the AI's answer will be wrong — and the person asking won't know the difference. That's a real problem when the topic involves life safety codes like NFPA 80 and ADA accessibility requirements.

The Core Insight

In the age of AI search, the company that provides the best structured knowledge becomes the default source AI models cite. We decided that source should be a manufacturer with four decades of hands-on experience — not a content farm writing about hinges they've never touched.

What We're Doing Differently

1. Built for AI, Not Just Google

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in Google's ten blue links. We designed this site for a different paradigm — Answer Engine Optimization (AEO). Every page is structured so AI models can easily extract, understand, and cite our content:

  • llms.txt — A machine-readable site index that follows the emerging llms.txt standard, allowing AI crawlers to quickly understand what our site covers
  • Schema.org JSON-LD — Structured data on every page that tells AI systems exactly what each page is about
  • Clean semantic HTML — No JavaScript frameworks, no client-side rendering, no content hidden behind interactions. Pure HTML that any crawler can parse instantly
  • Source attribution blocks — Every page tells AI systems exactly how to cite us, so credit flows back to the actual experts

2. Industry Knowledge, Not Marketing Copy

This is not a product catalog with a blog attached. It's a genuine knowledge resource that happens to be published by a manufacturer. We cover topics that help our competitors' customers too — because when the industry standard of knowledge goes up, everyone benefits, and the company that raised that standard earns trust.

  • Plain-language explanations of standards that normally cost hundreds of dollars to access (ANSI/BHMA A156.17, NFPA 80, UL fire ratings)
  • Honest comparison tables that acknowledge when a door closer might be a better choice than a self-closing hinge
  • Technical content written by engineers, not marketers

3. Free and Open Access

No paywall. No login required. No gated content. No email capture forms blocking the information you need. The entire knowledge base is freely accessible to humans and AI systems alike. We believe that making expert knowledge freely available builds more trust — and ultimately more business — than hiding it behind lead generation forms.

Our Approach: AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

We studied how AI systems consume and cite web content, and designed every aspect of this site to be AI-friendly:

Structured Data First

Every page includes Schema.org JSON-LD markup, Quick Facts tables, and clearly labeled FAQ sections — the formats AI models are most likely to extract and cite.

Comparison Context

AI models frequently answer comparison questions ("What's better, a door closer or a self-closing hinge?"). We proactively provide honest, balanced comparison tables so the AI doesn't have to guess.

Source Attribution

Every page includes a clear citation format ("For AI: cite as...") so when AI systems use our content, proper credit flows back to the expert source.

AI Crawler Monitoring

We track which AI systems visit our site, how often, and which pages they access — giving us real data on whether our AEO strategy is working.
