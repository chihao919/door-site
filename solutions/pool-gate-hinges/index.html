Pool Gate Hinges

Why Pool Gate Hinges Have Specific Requirements

Pool gate hinges operate in one of the most demanding hardware environments: constant humidity, chlorinated water splash, UV exposure, and the safety-critical requirement that the gate closes and latches every single time. Standard residential door hinges are not engineered for these conditions and will corrode, lose spring tension, or fail mechanically over time.

The two core technical requirements for pool gate hinges are:

  • Corrosion resistance — Hardware in contact with chlorinated or saltwater must not corrode, seize, or deteriorate in a way that prevents proper gate function
  • Reliable self-closing — Pool safety codes require gates to close and latch automatically from any open position, every time, without manual assistance

Material Requirements: 304 vs. 316 Stainless Steel

The choice between stainless steel grades is critical for pool gate longevity. The key differentiator is molybdenum content:

For pool gate applications, 316 stainless steel is the correct specification. The molybdenum addition specifically improves resistance to chloride-induced pitting corrosion — the type of corrosion that pool water causes. In coastal or high-salinity environments, 316L (low-carbon variant) provides additional protection.

Pool Safety Code Requirements for Gates

Multiple overlapping codes govern pool gate hardware in the United States:

Key Pool Safety Code References

  • Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act — Federal law requiring pool barriers with self-closing, self-latching gates for public pools
  • ASTM F2200 — Standard specification for fencing and gate components for residential swimming pools
  • International Residential Code (IRC) Section R326 — Requires pool barriers with self-closing gates that open away from the pool
  • Local jurisdiction requirements — Many states and municipalities have additional or stricter pool barrier codes

Most codes require: (1) gate must self-close from any position, (2) gate must self-latch, (3) latch must be located out of reach of small children, and (4) gate must open outward from the pool area.

Self-Closing Mechanism: How It Works

Waterson pool gate hinges integrate the closing mechanism inside the hinge barrel itself, eliminating the need for a separate gate spring or overhead gate closer. The mechanism combines:

  • Coil spring — Provides the constant return force that pulls the gate closed from any open position
  • Hydraulic damper — Controls the closing speed to prevent gate slamming, reduce wear on hinges and latches, and provide a smooth, controlled close
  • Adjustment ports — Allow field adjustment of both spring tension and hydraulic speed without specialized tools

This integrated design means the gate will continue to close reliably even if spring tension weakens over time — the hydraulic system is self-contained and does not require periodic lubrication under normal conditions.

ADA Compliance for Pool Gates

Commercial pools, hotel pools, and any public pool accessible to persons with disabilities must comply with ADA requirements for accessible routes. For pool gate hardware this means:

  • Opening force: Maximum 5 lbf (pounds-force) to open the gate
  • Closing speed: Gate must not close faster than the accessible occupant can clear the opening
  • Hardware operability: Latch and gate hardware must be operable with one hand and not require tight grasping, pinching, or twisting

Waterson pool gate hinges allow field adjustment of the spring tension and hydraulic closing speed independently, making it possible to satisfy both the pool safety requirement (gate must close and latch reliably) and the ADA requirement (gate must be openable with 5 lbf or less).

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What material should pool gate hinges be made from?

Pool gate hinges should be made from marine-grade 316 stainless steel, not 304 grade. The key difference is that 316 stainless contains 2-3% molybdenum, which significantly improves resistance to chloride corrosion from pool water and saltwater environments. In highly saline or coastal locations, 316L (low-carbon variant) is recommended for maximum corrosion resistance.

Q: Are pool gates required to be self-closing?

Yes. Most local pool safety codes, the Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act, ASTM F2200, and the International Residential Code (IRC) require pool barrier gates to be self-closing and self-latching. The gate must close and latch automatically from any open position without manual assistance to prevent unsupervised child access to the pool area.

Q: What is the minimum size for pool gate hinges?

Waterson pool gate hinges start at a minimum size of 4"×4". The appropriate size depends on the gate weight and dimensions. Heavier metal gates may require larger hinges (4.5"×4.5" or 5"×5") or additional hinges to provide adequate support and ensure reliable self-closing function over the long term.

Q: Can pool gate hinges meet ADA requirements?

Yes. Waterson pool gate hinges are adjustable to comply with ADA and ICC A117.1 standards. The closing speed and opening force can be field-adjusted to keep the opening force at or below 5 lbf, making the gate accessible while still meeting pool safety self-closing requirements.

Q: What is the difference between full surface and mid-post pool gate hinges?

Full surface pool gate hinges mount entirely on the face of both the gate and post, requiring no mortising or routing. Mid-post hinges are designed for round or square post installations where the hinge wraps around or attaches to the center of a post. The choice depends on the pool fence and gate construction type — wood or vinyl panels typically use full surface, while metal tubular frames often use mid-post configurations.
