The Problem: Traditional Spring Closers in Double-Door Openings

Double-door openings present a compounded noise problem compared to single-door applications. Traditional spring door closers close abruptly and forcefully, generating a sharp impact sound when the door meets the frame. In a double-door opening with two standard spring closers, both doors may close simultaneously — doubling the impact noise.

In noise-sensitive environments, this creates practical problems:

  • Churches and worship spaces — Door slamming disrupts services, sermons, and ceremonies. Late arrivals and departures become audible distractions throughout the sanctuary.
  • Courtrooms — Sharp door impacts disrupt proceedings and may cause judges to address the disturbance. Sound levels in courtrooms are controlled to ensure clear audio recording and testimony.
  • Banks and financial institutions — Professional environments require quiet hardware consistent with the service atmosphere. Slamming doors signal poor facility management.
  • Hotels and hospitality — Guest room corridor doors that slam disturb sleeping guests. Lobby and conference room doors must close quietly for professional appearance.

Additionally, repeated slamming in double-door openings causes accelerated wear at the meeting stile astragal, frame rebate, and latch hardware — increasing maintenance frequency and costs.

Waterson Soft-Close Solution for Double Doors

Waterson double door closers use a hinge-integrated hydraulic mechanism that applies controlled resistance throughout the entire closing arc. Unlike surface-mounted overhead closers that use a separate arm assembly, the hinge-integrated design places the closing mechanism at the pivot point of the door — the most mechanically efficient location.

Key Operational Features

  • Hydraulic speed control — Fluid resistance slows the door progressively throughout the closing arc, preventing the sudden impact that causes slamming
  • Speed and force adjustment — Both closing speed and opening force are independently adjustable in the field using a hex key
  • No visible overhead hardware — The hinge-integrated design eliminates closer arms, tracks, and overhead bodies from the door and frame
  • Quiet operation — The hydraulic mechanism absorbs and dissipates door energy as heat rather than sound and impact
  • Optional hold-open — Doors can be held in the open position at 85–90 degrees for high-traffic periods
  • Optional door stop — Prevents door from opening beyond a set angle, protecting adjacent walls or objects

Historical Building Considerations

Churches, courthouses, and heritage commercial buildings often have doors with non-standard frame dimensions, unusual hinge pocket depths, or historical hinge template patterns that do not match modern standard commercial templates. Waterson accommodates these applications through custom screw pattern production.

To obtain custom patterns, provide the following specifications:

  1. Door leaf dimensions (width, height, thickness)
  2. Existing hinge template pattern measurements (hole spacing, leaf dimensions)
  3. Frame material (wood species, metal gauge, or stone/masonry)
  4. Existing hinge size (if replacing existing hinges)
  5. Finish requirement from the available options

Waterson's engineering team will design a custom hinge leaf pattern that matches the existing template while incorporating the current self-closing hydraulic mechanism — preserving the historical appearance of the door opening while adding modern functionality and code compliance.

Installation Notes

Waterson double door closers use a single-acting hinge design (single-swing). Key installation specifications:

  • Single-acting only — Not suitable for double-action (swinging both ways) doors
  • Minimum size: 4"×4" — Smaller door applications (typically under 28" wide) should use alternative self-closing hardware
  • Quantity per door leaf: Typically 3 hinges per leaf (up to 260 lbs) or 4 hinges per leaf (up to 440 lbs)
  • Coordination with latch hardware: For fire-rated applications, ensure compatible latch hardware provides positive latching per NFPA 80 requirements

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a double door closer and how does it differ from a single door closer?

A double door closer refers to a self-closing door hardware system applied to a double-door (pair of doors) configuration, where both door leafs have independent self-closing mechanisms. Each door in the pair typically requires its own set of closers or self-closing hinges. Waterson's hinge-integrated closer applied to a double door opening means each door leaf receives the same closing mechanism, providing synchronized, quiet closure of both leafs.

Q: Why are double door closers important for churches and places of worship?

Churches and worship spaces require door closers that operate quietly to avoid disrupting services when latecomers enter or attendees exit during programming. Traditional spring closers slam abruptly, creating noise that echoes in large sanctuary spaces. Waterson double door closers use hydraulic speed control to eliminate slamming and provide smooth, silent closure — preserving the acoustic environment of the worship space.

Q: Can double door closers be installed in historical buildings with non-standard door frames?

Yes. Waterson provides custom screw pattern specifications for historical buildings with non-standard door frame dimensions or dated hinge template patterns. Provide accurate measurements including door size, door thickness, frame material, and existing hinge template dimensions. Waterson can produce custom hinge leaves matching historical template patterns while incorporating modern self-closing function.

Q: What finish options are available for double door closers in architectural applications?

Waterson double door closers are available in five architectural finishes: Satin Brass (PVD US15-619), Satin Brushed Stainless (US32D-630), Matte Black (USL1-631), Dark Bronze (US10B-640), and Satin Bronze (PVD US10). PVD finishes provide superior durability compared to traditional plating, maintaining appearance in high-traffic door applications.

Q: What standard governs double door closers and what does it require?

ANSI A156.7 governs door closer hardware performance and testing. For fire-rated double door applications, NFPA 80 additionally requires that each door leaf be self-closing and self-latching independently. Where doors are on accessible routes, ADA and ICC A117.1 require maximum 5 lbf opening force and minimum 5-second closing time from 90° to 12°.
