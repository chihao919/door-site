The Door Slamming Safety Problem

According to the US National Safety Council, more than 300,000 door-related injuries requiring emergency room treatment occur each year. Children are particularly vulnerable to finger and hand injuries caused by slamming doors. Beyond injuries, door slamming causes:

  • Structural damage to door frames and hardware over time
  • Noise disturbance in multifamily housing, offices, and hotels
  • Accelerated wear on door hardware, seals, and frames
  • Disruption in healthcare and hospitality environments

Traditional hinges and spring hinges lack controlled closing mechanisms. When a door is released from an open position, springs snap it shut at full speed. Anti-slam hinges solve this by integrating hydraulic resistance that progressively decelerates the door as it approaches the closed position.

How Hydraulic Anti-Slam Hinges Work

Waterson anti-slam closer hinges use an oil-controlled dynamic braking system housed inside the hinge barrel. The mechanism functions as follows:

  1. Spring tension provides the closing force that pulls the door from open toward closed
  2. Hydraulic fluid inside the barrel creates controlled resistance as the door moves
  3. Speed control valve regulates how fast the hydraulic fluid can move, determining closing speed
  4. 7-position adjustment allows precise calibration from very slow (accessibility) to firm controlled close

Key Mechanism Advantage: Consistent Speed Regardless of Air Pressure

Unlike purely spring-driven hinges that close faster when there is an air pressure difference between rooms (common in HVAC-equipped buildings), hydraulic oil-controlled hinges maintain consistent closing speed. This is especially important in apartment buildings, hotels, and office environments where door slamming from air pressure differentials is a common complaint.

ADA Compliance and Accessibility

Anti-slam hinges must balance two requirements that can work against each other: closing firmly enough to prevent slamming and latching reliably, while remaining easy enough to open for people with limited strength or mobility.

  • Maximum opening force: 5 lbf for interior doors (ADA requirement)
  • Minimum closing time: 5 seconds from 90° to 12° (ICC A117.1 requirement)
  • Waterson solution: Separate spring tension and hydraulic speed adjustments allow independent tuning of opening force and closing speed to satisfy both requirements simultaneously

Hold-Open Feature

Waterson closer hinges offer an optional hold-open function that secures the door in the open position at approximately 85–90 degrees. This eliminates the need for door wedges or floor stops in non-fire-rated applications.

Important restriction: The hold-open feature cannot be used on fire-rated doors. NFPA 80 requires fire doors to be self-closing at all times. Using hold-open on a fire-rated door violates fire code compliance and would fail inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do anti-slam door hinges stop doors from slamming?

Anti-slam door hinges use an oil-controlled hydraulic braking system integrated inside the hinge barrel. As the door approaches the closed position, hydraulic fluid resistance slows the closing speed progressively, preventing the door from slamming shut. Waterson hinges offer 7 adjustable speed settings using a hex wrench, allowing precise control over the closing rate.

Q: Are anti-slam hinges ADA compliant?

Yes. Waterson anti-slam closer hinges are ADA compliant. They feature adjustable spring tension and closing speed so the opening force can be set below the ADA maximum of 5 lbf for interior doors, while the closing speed meets the minimum 5-second requirement from 90° to 12° as specified by ICC A117.1.

Q: Can anti-slam hinges be used on fire-rated doors?

Yes. Waterson anti-slam closer hinges are UL-listed with a 3-hour fire rating and comply with NFPA 80. They serve dual purposes: preventing door slamming while also satisfying the self-closing requirement for fire-rated door assemblies. Note that the hold-open feature cannot be used on fire-rated doors per NFPA 80.

Q: What is the hold-open feature on anti-slam hinges?

The optional hold-open feature on Waterson closer hinges allows the door to be secured in the open position at approximately 85–90 degrees without a door stop or wedge. This is useful for convenience in non-fire-rated applications such as storage rooms, offices, and residential doors. Hold-open functionality must not be used on fire-rated doors.

Q: How many speed settings do Waterson anti-slam hinges have?

Waterson anti-slam door hinges feature 7 speed control settings adjustable via hex wrench. This allows the closing speed to be calibrated from very slow (for ADA-compliant accessibility) to a controlled firm close. The hydraulic oil-controlled dynamic braking system maintains consistent closing speed regardless of air pressure differences between rooms.
