What Is a 4-Inch Self-Closing Door Hinge?

A 4-inch self-closing door hinge is a combined closer-hinge unit where the automatic closing mechanism is built directly into the hinge barrel — rather than being a separate overhead closer mounted above the door. The "4 inch" refers to the height of the hinge leaf, which is the standard minimum size for Waterson's closer-hinge product line.

The K51M model integrates:

  • Spring mechanism — Provides the closing force that pulls the door shut
  • Hydraulic buffer — Controls closing speed to prevent slamming and meet ADA timing requirements
  • Adjustment panel — Numerical settings for both spring tension and hydraulic speed, adjustable with a standard hex key

This self-contained design eliminates the visible overhead closer arm and track, producing a cleaner aesthetic while meeting all code requirements for self-closing doors.

Hinge Count and Placement for 4-Inch Hinges

Correct hinge quantity and placement significantly affects the performance of self-closing hinges. Waterson recommends the following approach:

Installation Guidance

  • Start with 2 hinges on residential applications; the top hinge is the most important for generating closing torque
  • Add a 3rd hinge for heavier doors, stronger closing force requirements, or better long-term durability
  • Top hinge priority — When using 2 hinges, ensure the top hinge is a self-closing model; the bottom can be a standard hinge
  • All hinges as self-closing when using 3+ hinges for best performance and balanced load distribution

On fire-rated door assemblies, three self-closing hinges are typically required to meet NFPA 80 performance standards for heavier fire-rated doors.

ADA Compliance and Closing Speed Adjustment

Residential doors on accessible routes — such as apartment entry doors or accessible unit entry doors — must comply with ADA and ICC A117.1. The Waterson K51M self-closing hinge satisfies these requirements through its dual-adjustment system:

  • Spring tension adjustment — Reduces opening force to below the ADA maximum of 5 lbf for interior doors
  • Hydraulic speed adjustment — Slows closing to meet the ICC A117.1 minimum of 5 seconds from 90° to 12°

These two adjustments work independently, allowing installers to meet both the force limit (for accessibility) and the closing time minimum (for self-closing function and fire code) simultaneously on the same door.

Fire Rating and NFPA 80 Compliance

The Waterson K51M 4-inch self-closing hinge is UL-listed with a 3-hour fire rating — the highest available for self-closing hinges. This makes it suitable for any fire-rated door assembly from 20-minute to 3-hour ratings, including:

  • Residential unit entry doors in apartment buildings
  • Fire-rated door between attached garage and living space
  • Stairwell and corridor fire doors in multifamily buildings
  • Mechanical room and utility space fire-rated doors

Important: when using the 4-inch K51M on fire-rated assemblies, the hold-open feature must not be activated. NFPA 80 requires fire doors to self-close and latch from any open position without being held open.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a 4-inch self-closing door hinge?

A 4-inch self-closing door hinge is a combined closer-hinge unit that integrates an automatic door closing mechanism into a standard hinge body. The 4-inch measurement refers to the hinge leaf height. Unlike a traditional door hinge that only pivots, a self-closing hinge contains an internal spring and/or hydraulic system that automatically pulls the door shut after it is opened, eliminating the need for a separate overhead door closer.

Q: How many 4-inch self-closing hinges do I need for a door?

Waterson recommends starting with two self-closing hinges for residential applications and adding a third if needed for heavier doors or stronger closing action. The top hinge placement is most critical for self-closing function — it generates the most return torque. For doors over 200 lbs or commercial applications, three hinges are standard, with four available for doors up to 440 lbs.

Q: Are 4-inch self-closing hinges ADA compliant?

Yes, when properly adjusted. ADA requires interior door closing force under 5 lbf and closing time of at least 5 seconds from 90° to 12° per ICC A117.1. The Waterson K51M 4-inch self-closing hinge features adjustable hydraulic speed control and spring tension that can be field-set to meet both requirements simultaneously.

Q: Can 4-inch self-closing hinges be used on fire-rated doors?

Yes. Waterson 4-inch self-closing hinges carry a UL-listed 3-hour fire rating and comply with NFPA 80 self-closing requirements. They are an approved alternative to overhead door closers on fire-rated door assemblies. Note: the hold-open feature must not be installed on fire-rated doors, as NFPA 80 requires fire doors to close and latch from any open position.

Q: What corner options are available for 4-inch self-closing door hinges?

Waterson 4-inch self-closing hinges are available with 1/4-inch radius corners or 5/8-inch radius corners (to match existing door or frame preparations), in both zig-zag and architectural arch screw patterns. The door side and frame side can have different corner profiles for custom applications. Standard lead time is approximately 30 days for non-standard corner combinations.
